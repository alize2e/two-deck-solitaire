Solitaire

Hello! Welcome to this two-deck game of solitaire. Similar to Klondike solitaire, this one instead uses two decks of cards, and has slightly different rules. I learned this solitaire from friends, and may have inadvertently changed the rules over the years, so I don't know its name nor if it exactly corresponds to a formal solitaire. A big thank-you to those friends, and to all my beta testers!

Rules

Goal

  • Stack all the cards by suit in ascending order in the top 8 columns.

Quick Start

  • If you see an ace, move it to one of the top piles. Start placing smaller cards on larger ones of the opposite color, or a sub-stack of cards on a larger card of the opposite color. When you can no longer stack, press 'd', and repeat until you hopefully win. For more details, including on how to move cards, read on; or start playing now – you won't be able to move any cards where you aren't allowed to.

Sorting

  • Top middle pile:
    You cannot stack cards here. The visible card is always accessible.

  • Other top stacks:
    In the 8 other top stacks, cards are stacked by suit in ascending order (the ace is the smallest card). All of the top stacks are always entirely sorted, and the topmost card in each stack is accessible.

  • Bottom columns:
    In contrast, in the bottom 10 columns, for cards to be sorted, they must be ordered in descending order with alternating colors (i.e. you can only stack a red card on a black one and vice versa). Furthermore, neither the visible nor hidden parts of columns are necessarily completely sorted.
    A card is considered to be accessible if its face is visible and the column is sorted, at least between that card and the bottom.

Play

  • Moving cards:
    Click on a card to select it, and then hover over the card you want to place it on to put the card down. To deselect a card, move the mouse off of the card and then hover over it without clicking. (If you have trouble moving a card, make sure you aren't accidentally deselecting it.)

  • Placing on a bottom column:
    You can place any accessible card – plus the cards stacked on it – on a bottom column if: the column is empty; or the value of the card in question is one lower than that of the topmost card and those two cards have different colors.

  • Stacking on a top stack:
    You can stack an accessible card that does not have cards placed on top of it if the stack in question would remain sorted.

  • Moving a card from the middle pile:
    You can only remove cards from the top middle pile by stacking on a top stack, and said stack must remain sorted.

  • Dealing cards:
    Click 'd', and a card will be added, face-up, to each bottom column that does not have a king at the top. When there are no cards left to be added, the background will turn green. If all the cards have been dealt (the screen will turn orange) and you cannot win: reload to try again, if you have the Patience.

  • Rules:
    Click 'r' to toggle between seeing and hiding the rules. Don't worry too much about the rules, because you won't be able to move any cards where you aren't allowed to. Good luck!
